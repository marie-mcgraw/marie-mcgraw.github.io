    About Me

    I am an atmospheric and climate scientist studying climate variability and climate change. Currently, I am studying extreme sea ice loss on daily and weekly timescales with Cecilia Bitz and Ed Blanchard-Wrigglesworth. Previously, my research has encompassed topics such as links between Arctic and midlatitude weather, high-latitude moisture transport, jet stream variability and climate change, and large-scale circulation impacts of volcanic eruptions. I have experience working with idealized and fully-coupled climate models, forecast models, and reanalysis products. In addition to studying the atmosphere and the climate, I am also interested in the statistical and analytical tools we as scientists use to study and explore our wealth of measurements and data.
