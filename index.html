Marie McGraw

Postdoctoral Research Associate, Atmospheric Sciences, Colorado State University | Cooperative Institute for Research in the Atmosphere
Update: After 2 great years at the University of Washington, I have returned to Colorado to become part of the National Science Foundation's AI Institute for Research on Trustworthy AI in Weather, Climate, and Coastal Oceanography (AI2ES). I am an atmospheric and climate scientist studying climate variability and climate change. My research uses idealized and fully-coupled climate models, forecast models, and reanalysis products to study topics such as extreme sea ice loss on day-to-day timescales, links between Arctic and midlatitude weather and climate, jet stream variability and climate change, and large-scale circulation impacts of volcanic eruptions. In addition to studying the atmosphere and the climate, I am also interested in the statistical and analytical tools we as scientists use to study and explore our wealth of measurements and data.
